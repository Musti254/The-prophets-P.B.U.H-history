Introduction

Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) is the final prophet in Islam. Muslims believe he was sent as a mercy to all of humanity, bringing the message of Tawhid (the oneness of Allah), justice, compassion, and moral integrity.

Why His Life Matters

The life of the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) serves as a complete guide for Muslims. His patience in hardship, honesty in dealings, mercy toward others, and devotion to Allah are timeless lessons that continue to inspire billions around the world today.

Core Values He Taught

  • Belief in One God (Allah)
  • Truthfulness and trust
  • Kindness to parents, neighbors, and the poor
  • Justice and equality
  • Patience and forgiveness

Early Life

Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) was born in Makkah around the year 570 CE. He was orphaned at a young age and raised by his grandfather and later his uncle. Even before prophethood, he was known among his people as Al-Ameen (the Trustworthy) because of his honesty and noble character.

The First Revelation

At the age of 40, while meditating in the Cave of Hira, the Angel Jibreel (Gabriel) brought the first revelation from Allah. This marked the beginning of his mission as the final messenger, calling people to worship Allah alone and abandon injustice and corruption.

Message and Legacy

Despite facing strong opposition, persecution, and hardship, the Prophet (ﷺ) remained patient and steadfast. His message eventually spread across Arabia and beyond, shaping a civilization rooted in faith, knowledge, and moral responsibility.
